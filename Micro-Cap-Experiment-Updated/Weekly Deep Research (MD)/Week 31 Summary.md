## Per-Position Thesis

**MRAM (Everspin Technologies) — Conviction: 4/5**

MRAM remains the portfolio's highest-conviction position despite the required 1-share trim for concentration compliance. The thesis rests on three pillars: (1) the Microchip Technology manufacturing partnership announced April 8, 2026, which validates on-shore supply chain scalability, (2) the 128Mb AEC-Q100 automotive qualification expected May 2026 that unlocks a new addressable market in ADAS and EV power management, and (3) 238 design wins in 2025 (up from 178) creating a multi-year revenue pipeline.

Q1 2026 earnings on April 29 are the near-term test. Revenue is guided to $14.0-15.0M with non-GAAP EPS of $0.07-0.12. The stock has run +20.6% from entry, and the +30% partial profit threshold ($13.34) is only 7.8% away. If MRAM beats and guides higher on auto qualification progress, the stock could hit $13.34 and trigger the first partial take.

**Risk:** If revenue disappoints or the Microchip partnership is delayed, the stock could retrace to the $10.98 stop. At 9 shares post-trim, max loss is $12.51 (3.1% of equity) — within risk budget.

**ORN (Orion Group Holdings) — Conviction: 4/5**

ORN's thesis strengthened this week. Grace & White Inc. accumulated a $9.31M position as of April 17, confirming institutional conviction. The infrastructure tailwind is structural — $125M+ in recent contract wins, $900-950M revenue guidance (up from $852M in 2025), and Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $54-58M. Earnings estimate revisions remain positive.

Q1 earnings on April 28 will test whether the backlog converts to revenue growth. The stock bounced +4% Friday to $11.97, recovering from mid-week weakness. The +0.8% gain from entry is modest, but the thesis is fundamentally about the multi-quarter infrastructure spending cycle, not a single-quarter outcome.

**Risk:** Marine construction is weather-sensitive. A weak Q1 due to storms or project delays could cause a temporary setback. Stop at $11.12 limits loss to $5.95 (1.5% of equity).

**ECVT (Ecovyst) — Conviction: 3/5 (downgraded from prior week)**

ECVT's Friday -4.37% drop is concerning but not thesis-breaking. **Risk:** If the stock continues lower Monday, the $13.24 stop could trigger before earnings. This would be an acceptable outcome given defensive posture — let the stop manage risk rather than adding discretionary intervention.
